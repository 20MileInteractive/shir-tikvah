Early Childhood Programs

Is it ever too early to begin?
At Shir Tikvah, we think not. Our early childhood programs are excellent jumping off points for families that want to explore what it’s like to get involved in a Jewish community — perhaps for the first time — and to meet other families who are doing the same.

Tot Shabbat
Tot Shabbat is a good place to start. Tot Shabbat is an early introduction to Jewish fun for infants, toddlers, pre-K kids, and their families. These lively get-togethers are a mix of singing, dancing, and story telling, followed by snacks and simple, age-appropriate craft projects. Tot Shabbats can be attended on a drop-in basis; temple membership is not required.

Our Experiencing Jewish Life program (it’s a mouthful; we call it EJL) is for three- to five-year-olds and kindergartners focusing on the Jewish holidays. Children and parents start the morning singing songs and getting comfortable. Parents then go off to a parallel learning session of their own, and kids continue on in a pre-school or kindergarten class with their teachers.

EJL helps families celebrate and explore Jewish holidays and related rituals, build a repertoire of Jewish experiences at home, and create a community for parents with kids of the same age.

Membership is not required, but preference is given to members. This program fills up fast, so register early.
