Our Building

It’s hard to believe, but our temple building used to be a Knights of Columbus and an American Legion hall! It took a lot of imagination — but with financial support from many generous members, a loan from a bank, and much creativity, the building was renovated, a porch added overlooking Wedge Pond, and a beautiful garden and surrounding grounds nurtured.

Like all Shir Tikvah projects, the work involved many talented members who contributed to the renovation, including the interior frieze, the carvings on the Bimah, and the design of the building itself, which recalls shuls common to many Eastern European villages in the nineteenth century. The inside space is light and airy. Learn more about the building.

We love our temple home because it reflects who we are: welcoming and down-to-earth.
